The Spectacular Scene in March

Choto had lived all his life in March, a quiet land where the wind smelled of fresh soil and the mornings were always calm. He knew every path, every tree, and every small sound the land made at night. March was not just a place to Choto—it was his home, and it knew him just as well. One evening, as the sun dipped low and painted the sky in soft orange light, a mysterious stranger arrived. The stranger wore worn clothes and carried nothing but tired eyes and a long silence. No one in March had seen him before. Choto did not ask many questions. He looked at the stranger and felt something gentle in his heart. “You may stay,” Choto said. Choto gave the stranger a small piece of land near the river, where the grass grew tall and the soil was good. He shared his food—warm bread, fresh vegetables, and clean water. He even brought extra blankets so the stranger would not feel cold at night. The stranger worked hard and slowly built a small home. Each day, he looked less tired. Each night, a small fire glowed near his shelter, and the land of March felt warmer somehow. Over time, the stranger smiled more. He helped Choto tend the land, fix broken fences, and plant new seeds. Though he remained quiet about his past, his actions spoke loudly. Kindness had given him a new beginning. March changed that year. It became fuller, richer—not because of new land or wealth, but because kindness had made room for another life. And Choto, who had always lived in March, learned that sharing his home made it grow even bigger.
